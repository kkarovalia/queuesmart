# 1. Initial Thoughts

The main users of QueueSmart would be people who are on a waitlist at restaurants. These users may be customers who walk in and want the next available table, or customers who want to choose a preferred date and time for a reservation. The administrators would be restaurant staff or managers who are responsible for managing tables, reservations, and the customer waitlist.

Users would interact with the application by selecting their party size, choosing a preferred date and time, selecting a preferred table if that option is available, or simply choosing the next available spot in the queue. They would also be able to view their position in the queue and see an estimated wait time. Administrators would interact with the application by providing information about the restaurant's tables, viewing how many customers are currently in the queue, managing future reservations, and reviewing analytics on how accurate the wait time estimates are.

The most important features of the system would be wait time estimation, cancellation tracking, restaurant volume indicators for reservations, and showing customers their position in the queue. These features are important because they help customers understand how long they may need to wait and help restaurants manage seating more efficiently.

Some challenges we anticipate include creating accurate wait time estimations, especially when table turnover times change. We also expect challenges with handling cancellations, walk-ins, and long queues during busy times. Another challenge is optimizing the backend so the system can update queue information quickly and reliably. Finally, the application should have an intuitive and appealing user experience so both customers and restaurant staff can use it easily.
